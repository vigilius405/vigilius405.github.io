Generative machine learning for biology

Hi! My name’s Pierce—I recently graduated from the University of Chicago in quantitative biology, and am now headed to Cornell Tech for a masters in computer science. I’m passionate about the use of machine learning and computational modeling to expedite biological discovery and pharmaceutical development. On this website, you can find my various experiences, projects, and skills. Don’t hesitate to reach out!

Experience

  • Learning Latent Design Spaces in Protein Generative Models

    In the Ranganathan Lab at UChicago, I completed my Bachelor of Science thesis with Research Honors. I designed novel variational autoencoder-based models for protein representation learning and generation, with a focus on latent space disentanglement of protein functional properties. I was able to improve functional awareness of the latent space and get closer to achieving tunable protein properties.
    Read my thesis

  • Implicit Neural Representations

    For a Neural Networks course, I experimented with implicit neural representation and wrote my final project on Evaluation of Out-of-Bounds Pattern Conservation in Implicit Neural Representation Networks. I found that Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks were particularly good at generalization and expansion of complex implicit patterns.
    Read the paper

  • UChicago iGEM 2024 (VIPR)

    The UChicago Genehackers team won Gold at the international Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) 2024 competition in Paris for development of a schistosomiasis vaccine candidate now under animal testing at the NIH. Alongside helping with project conceptualization, I raised $40,000 for research and travel costs and managed lab finances.
    Visit the website

  • Quantitative Biosciences Center
